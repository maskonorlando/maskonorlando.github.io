University of Chicago
Research Internship

Completed a 10-week Data Science project that used R, Python and MatLab.
Findings were presented at SPIE Medical Imaging 2020.

During Summer 2019, I had the opportunity to take part in MEDical Informatics eXperiences, an NSF REU site. Under the guidance of Dr. Armato, I analyzed a dataset from the INSPIRE cohort, to understand if radiomic features from abdominal chest CT scans of patients who have idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis can predict survival or genetic composition. During the process, I filtered the dataset to include 169 patients, updated software to extract information from certain regions of interest, applied various machine learning techniques for analysis and compiled multiple presentations. My research was presented at SPIE 2020 Medical Imaging Conference along with an extended abstract that I co-wrote.
